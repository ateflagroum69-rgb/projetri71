Aile Moderne

Chasseurs ayant fait leurs débuts à l'ère moderne, le 17 novembre 2000 ou après


Robbie Lawler

Fort d'une carrière de 22 ans en MMA et de 47 combats à son actif, Lawler affiche un palmarès de 30 victoires, 16 défaites et 1 sans décision (19-15 à l'UFC, au PRIDE et au Strikeforce). Il a notamment vaincu des figures emblématiques telles que Donald Cerrone (membre du Hall of Fame de l'UFC), Frank Trigg (Fight Wing), l'ancien champion des poids welters de l'UFC Johny Hendricks, l'ancien champion intérimaire des poids welters de l'UFC Carlos Condit, l'ancien champion des poids welters du Strikeforce et du WEC Nick Diaz, et l'ancien champion des poids mi-lourds du WEC Scott Smith. Lawler est devenu champion des poids welters de l'UFC en 2014, après sa victoire face à Johnny Hendricks lors de leur revanche très attendue à l'UFC 181. Son statut de légende s'est confirmé lors de ses deux combats suivants, puisqu'il a défendu son titre des poids welters contre Rory MacDonald et Carlos Condit à l'UFC 189 (2015) et à l'UFC 195 (2016). Ces deux combats furent de véritables guerres en cinq rounds, récompensées par le titre de Combat de l'Année décerné par plusieurs médias. En 2023, son combat contre MacDonald à l'UFC 189 fut intronisé au Temple de la renommée de l'UFC (section Combat). Au cours de sa carrière, il a également remporté sept victoires par KO et porté plus de 1 200 coups significatifs chez les poids welters.

Amanda Nunes

Forte de 28 combats en 15 ans de carrière professionnelle en MMA, Nunes affiche un palmarès de 23 victoires pour 5 défaites (17-3 à l'UFC/Strikeforce). Elle a notamment vaincu Ronda Rousey, membre du Hall of Fame de l'UFC, Valentina Shevchenko (à deux reprises), championne des poids mouches de l'UFC, Julianna Peña, championne des poids coqs de l'UFC, Germaine de Randamie (à deux reprises) et Cris Cyborg, anciennes championnes des poids plumes de l'UFC, ainsi que Miesha Tate, Holly Holm et Raquel Pennington, anciennes championnes des poids coqs de l'UFC. -Elle est la première femme à être devenue championne dans deux catégories de poids et à détenir simultanément les titres UFC des poids coqs et des poids plumes. Shogun Rua

A veteran of 42 fights during his 21-year professional MMA career, Rua registered a record of 27-14-1 (23-13-1 – UFC / PRIDE), securing notable wins over UFC Hall of Famers Mark Coleman, Chuck Liddell, Kevin Randleman, and Forrest Griffin, former UFC light heavyweight champions Quinton “Rampage” Jackson and Lyoto Machida, and former Strikeforce® heavyweight champion Alistair Overeem. Outside the Octagon, Rua has won several awards during his career, receiving Knockout of the Year honors in 2010 during the Fighters Only World MMA Awards for his bout against Lyoto Machida at UFC 113. In 2005, he received Fight of the Year for his bout against Antonio Rogerio Nogueira at PRIDE FC: Critical Countdown 2005. UFC Light Heavyweight champion (2010-2011) 2005 Pride Middleweight Grand Prix champion 8 post-fight bonuses (second-most in UFC light heavyweight history)

Joanna Jędrzejczyk

A veteran of 21 fights during her 10-year professional MMA career, Joanna compiled a record of 16-5 (10-5 UFC), securing victories over former UFC strawweight champions Carla Esparza and Jessica Andrade. She is the second female inductee in UFC history and first from the strawweight division. Outside the Octagon, Joanna has been honored with numerous awards throughout her career, receiving Female Fighter of the Year in 2015 by MMA Junkie. Joanna’s unforgettable 2020 fight against then-UFC strawweight champion Zhang Weili during UFC 248 won Fight of the Year honors from MMA Junkie, MMA Fighting, BT Sport, The Athletic, and the Fighters Only World MMA Awards. A native of Olsztyn, Poland, Joanna began competing in Muay Thai at the age of 16. She competed in 40 amateur Muay Thai fights, compiling a 37-3 record before turning pro. Her only three losses were to future UFC flyweight champion Valentina Shevchenko. As an amateur, she captured four consecutive gold medals (2009-13) and a silver medal (2008) during the IFMA World Championships. As a professional Muay Thai and kickboxer, she fought in 31 matches, registering a 27-3 record with one draw, and became the World Kickboxing Federation’s European champion in 2010. A philanthropist in her native Poland, Joanna is an ambassador for several children’s hospitals and food banks, raising funds and awareness for families in need. UFC strawweight champion (2015-2017) First female European UFC champion First Polish UFC champion 6 Title Fight Wins (Most in UFC strawweight history)


Frankie Edgar

Before the world knew who Frankie Edgar was, the former UFC lightweight champion was just another kid from Jersey with dreams of making a living as a prizefighter. Those dreams took him to the Jerome Boxing Club in the Bronx in July of 2005, where the 23-year-old was going to compete in a mixed martial arts fight that bore little resemblance to the ones he would experience inside the UFC. MMA wasn’t even sanctioned in New York, leading to underground shows. For those who stepped into the ring, it wasn’t about money or championship belts, it was all about the fight. So Edgar packed his mouthpiece and took the drive from Toms River with his team and his father, as well as his future wife Renee, on a journey that would see him becoming a world champion and legend of the sport. UFC Lightweight Champion (2010-2012) 8 Fight of the Night Bonuses (Second-most in UFC history) 7:57:10 Total Fight Time (Second-most in UFC history)

Donald "Cowboy" Cerrone was a unique personality, a permanent fixture on the main card and in the Top 15, and a perpetually entertaining fighter who really and truly was willing to fight anyone, anywhere, at any time. He was one of a kind. Khabib Nurmagomedov

It didn’t take long for Nurmagomedov to become the world’s best. By 2018, he was a world champion and a little over two years later, the question wasn’t whether he was the best lightweight in the world, but if he was the best to ever strap on the four-ounce gloves. While some competitors like the pomp and circumstance that goes along with a big fight, relish the trash talk and the gladiatorial sense of it all, Khabib Nurmagomedov just wanted to win, represent his team, and make his father proud. Inducted July 2022 UFC Lightweight Champion (2018-2021) Retired undefeated (29-0) 2021 ESPY Winner – Best MMA Fighter


Daniel Cormier

Daniel Cormier is one of the few fighters to earn double champ status within the UFC Octagon, claiming belts in both the light heavyweight and heavyweight division. Cormier successfuly defended the light heavyweight title three times, defeating Anthony 'Rumble' Johnson and Alexander Gustaffson. He defeated Stipe Moicic at UFC 226 to win the heavyweight title and successfully defended it once against Derrick Lewis. Cormier is the first fighter to defend titles in two weight classes and succeed. The Louisana native retired in 2020 and was inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame in July 2022. Inducted July 2022 UFC Heavyweight Champion (2018-2019) UFC Light Heavyweight Champion (2015-2018) Two-time US Olympian

Georges St-Pierre

Both in and out of the Octagon, Georges St-Pierre was a clear pick for the UFC Hall of Fame, and as the dust has settled on his time as an active fighter, the never-ending debate has kicked up again, with fans and pundits arguing about who the greatest mixed martial artist of all-time is. It’s impossible to have that debate without St-Pierre in the conversation, and as the years go by, his case gets stronger and stronger. A veteran of 28 fights during his 17-year career, St-Pierre compiled a record of 26-2 (20-2, UFC), which included wins over UFC Hall of Famers BJ Penn, Matt Hughes, Matt Serra and Michael Bisping. Inducted September 2021 UFC Middleweight Champion (2017) Two-time UFC welterweight champion (2006-2007, 2008-2013) Three-time Canadian Athlete of the Year (2008, 2009, 2010)

Rashad Evans

Rashad Evans was a trailblazer as one of the most successful Black athletes in the early years of UFC. During his storied career from 2005 through 2018 in UFC, Evans held a 14-8-1 record, earning wins against fellow legends of the sport along the way. Evans won the second season of The Ultimate Fighter, defeating Brad Imes in the finale. At UFC 92 in 2008, he defeated Forrest Griffin to win the light heavyweight championship. Inducted July 2019 UFC Light Heavyweight Champion (2008-2009) The Ultimate Fighter Season Two Winner

Michael Bisping

From the early days of The Ultimate Fighter, it was clear that Michael Bisping was a fighters fighter. "The Count" was an all-action athlete both in the Octagon, on the mic, and on screen. He became the first British fighter to become a UFC champion and his epic bouts will live on forever in the UFC Hall of Fame. Inducted July 2019 UFC Middleweight Champion (2016-2017) The Ultimate Fighter Season Three Winner Pioneer of UK MMA


Ronda Rousey

Very few names in MMA have transcended the sport quite like “Rowdy” Ronda, a true trailblazer for woman in combat sports. Even after her fighting career came to an end, Rousey made history by becoming the first woman to be inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame. Rousey has continued to entertain fans since retiring by transitioning to acting and professional wrestling. And she continued to make history by competing in the first women’s match to headline a Wrestlemania event. Learn more about this pioneering figure in mixed martial arts. Inducted July 2018 First-ever women’s UFC champion UFC Bantamweight Champion (2012-2015)

Urijah Faber

Urijah Faber's legacy can’t be reduced to wins and losses, though he did defeat the likes of Dominick Cruz, Jeff Curran, Jens Pulver, Raphael Assuncao, Eddie Wineland and Brian Bowles. A former WEC featherweight champion from 2006 to 2008, he engaged in SuperFights with Pulver, Cruz, Jose Aldo, Renan Barao and Frankie Edgar, and established himself as one of the best of this era. Yet what Faber should be remembered for most is his efforts in exposing the lighter weight classes to the United States audience. As WEC champion after the Zuffa purchase of the promotion, Faber was the one primarily tasked with talking with the media, making appearances, and then stepping into the cage on fight night and wrecking hungry challengers. Forrest Griffin

The beauty of Forrest Griffin is that he doesn’t put too much stock in drama, but he’s been a part of so many dramatic moments in the UFC Octagon. His epic three round battle against Stephan Bonnar in the first TUF finale in April of 2005 put mixed martial arts on the map. He isn’t prone to waxing poetically about the emotional highs and lows of life in the fight game, yet he’s worn his heart on his sleeve and bared himself to the world on a more than a few occasions. And that’s fine, considering that when the bell rang, he wasn't holding anything back. He is as raw as it gets in this sport, and while raw isn’t always pretty, there is something so compelling and pure about it that we can’t turn away. Inducted July 2013 UFC Light Heavyweight Champion (2008) The Ultimate Fighter Season One Winner
